Network Modulation

Analog modulation

Amplitude modulation is a process by which the wave signal is transmitted by modulating the amplitude of the signal. It is often called AM and is commonly used in transmitting a piece of information through a radio carrier wave.

Digital modulation

Digital modulation is the process of encoding a digital information signal into the amplitude, phase, or frequency of the transmitted signal. The encoding process affects the bandwidth of the transmitted signal and its robustness to channel impairments.

Pulse modulation

Pulse modulation is a type of modulation technique used in communication systems to transmit analog signals over a digital communication channel. In pulse modulation, the analog signal is sampled at regular intervals and each sample is converted into a series of pulses, which are then transmitted over the channel.
